Whisper

Personal Hygiene: Regular bathing and cleaning of the body, including the genital area, is fundamental. Use mild soap and water to clean the genitals and avoid harsh products that can irritate sensitive skin. After using the toilet, wiping from front to back can prevent bacteria from the anus from spreading to the urethra and vagina. Safe Sexual Practices: Using barriers such as condoms and dental dams during sexual activity can significantly reduce the risk of STIs. It's important to use these barriers correctly and consistently. After intercourse, both partners should urinate and gently wash their genitals to remove potential irritants or pathogens. Menstrual Hygiene: During menstruation, changing sanitary products regularly, at least every 4-6 hours, is crucial to prevent infections and odor. Choose products that suit your body and flow, and always wash your hands before and after handling menstrual products. Undergarments: Wear clean, breathable underwear made from natural fabrics like cotton. Change underwear daily, or more frequently if you sweat a lot or after exercising. Avoid tight-fitting clothing that can trap moisture and create a breeding ground for bacteria.

Oral Hygiene: Oral health is also a part of sexual hygiene, especially if engaging in oral sex. Brushing teeth twice a day, flossing, and using mouthwash can reduce the risk of transmitting infections through the mouth.
